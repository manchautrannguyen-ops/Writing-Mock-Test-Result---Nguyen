Task 2 · Discuss both views and give your opinion
Q
Exam Task
Task Prompt Scientists agree that many people eat too much junk food and it is damaging their health. Some people think that this problem can be solved by education, while others believe that this education will not work. Discuss both views and give your opinion.
✍
Candidate's Essay
Written by Nguyên

Nowadays, junk food or fast food has become popular among young people. It's very convenient and cheap, easily found everywhere. But it is unhealthy, because it has many harm lipid, less protein and energy. Some people believe this problem can be solved by education, while others believe that this education will not work. In my opinion, education about the disadvantages of junk food is necessary, but the way to instruct the young people to understand clearly is important.

The first, the young people need know about the reason of avoiding the fast food. Recently, the rate of children get the chronic disease is increase fastly, such as diabetes, hypertension, or renal failure. The cause of this disease can start by obesity or gain weight of young people. They often use fast food and get away exercises. So, it lead to they easily get many ill asocciated with obesity.

The second, we need find the best way to educate the child know and actively avoid the junk food. In every family, the parents must spend a lot of times for communicating with their childs. Besides, they can go out with the childrents, and participating the workshop about exercises as swimming, climping and running.

Lastly, the education is not only stop at learn in book or at school, but also in life per day, such as communication in family, or take part in work together outside. Fast food is not good for health, but the way of education the young people to active avoid junk food is reality important.

Scores & Feedback by Criterion
Task Response
4

The essay attempts to address the topic but largely misses the task requirement. A "discuss both views and give your opinion" question requires the writer to present the case for education solving the problem and the case against it, then defend a personal position.

Instead, this essay mostly argues how to educate young people about junk food, which is a different question entirely. The opposing view — that education will not work — is never meaningfully discussed. The personal opinion stated in the introduction ("the way to instruct young people is important") is vague and does not engage with the actual debate. Ideas are present but underdeveloped and sometimes tangential, such as the suggestion to go swimming and climbing. Read Bình's essays to learn how to properly develop relevant ideas for this task.

Coherence & Cohesion
4

There is a basic paragraph structure, and the writer attempts to use sequencing markers: the first, the second, lastly. However, these are used as standalone openers in a non-standard way ("The first" rather than "First" or "Firstly") and function more as a list than as academic discourse markers.

Internal paragraph logic is weak — the second paragraph moves from chronic disease rates to obesity to exercise without clear logical connections between ideas. The third paragraph shifts abruptly from family communication to outdoor workshops with no linking explanation. Referencing is sometimes unclear or absent.

Lexical Resource
4

The vocabulary range is limited and there are frequent errors in word choice and word formation that sometimes impede meaning. Key examples:

  • "many harm lipid" → "harmful lipids"
  • "is increase fastly" → "is increasing rapidly / has been rising rapidly"
  • "get many ill" → "suffer from many illnesses"
  • "asocciated" → "associated"
  • "climping" → "climbing"
  • "childrents," "childs" → "children"
  • "spend a lot of times" → "spend a lot of time"
  • "life per day" → "daily life"
  • "reality important" → "really important"

These errors are numerous and consistent, and several cause genuine difficulty for the reader. The writer appears to be working at the edge of their vocabulary range throughout. Grammatical Range & Accuracy
4

Sentence structures are mostly simple, and attempts at more complex constructions break down regularly. Key grammatical problems include:

  • "the young people need know" → "need to know"
  • "the rate of children get the chronic disease is increase fastly" → "the rate at which children develop chronic diseases is increasing rapidly"
  • "It lead to they easily get" → "It leads to them easily developing"
  • "is not only stop at learn in book" → "not limited to learning from books"

Subject-verb agreement is inconsistent throughout. Punctuation is generally adequate at the sentence level but the apostrophe in "It's" is inappropriate for formal academic writing. Errors are frequent enough to impede meaning in several places. Bài luận có cố gắng đề cập đến chủ đề nhưng phần lớn không đáp ứng đúng yêu cầu đề. Dạng bài "discuss both views and give your opinion" yêu cầu người viết trình bày cả hai phía: (1) giáo dục có thể giải quyết vấn đề và (2) giáo dục không thể giải quyết vấn đề, sau đó đưa ra và bảo vệ quan điểm cá nhân.

Tuy nhiên, bài này chủ yếu tập trung vào việc nên giáo dục người trẻ như thế nào về đồ ăn nhanh (lạc đề). Quan điểm "giáo dục không hiệu quả" hầu như không được đề cập và triển khai. Most Important Issues to Address
  • 1

    Answer the actual question.

    This is the most urgent priority. Before writing, identify exactly what the task is asking. Here, the question asks you to discuss two specific views — that education can solve the junk food problem, and that it cannot — and then give your own opinion on which is more convincing. Planning two to three minutes before writing to map out what each paragraph will argue would make a significant difference.

  • 2

    Verb tense and subject-verb agreement.

    Errors like "the rate is increase fastly" and "it lead to they" appear throughout and are patterns that need focused grammar practice — particularly present simple vs. present continuous, and how to use infinitives after modal/auxiliary verbs (need to know, lead to them developing).

  • 3

    Word formation accuracy.

    Many errors come from incorrect suffixes and word class confusion — harmful not harm (adjective vs. noun), rapidly not fastly (adverb), illnesses not ill (noun vs. adjective). Studying common word families (noun/verb/adjective/adverb forms) for high-frequency IELTS vocabulary would directly address this.

  • 4

    Avoid informal contractions.

    "It's" should be "It is" in formal academic writing. Contractions are appropriate in spoken English and informal writing, but not in IELTS essays.

  • 1

    Trả lời đúng yêu cầu đề bài.

    Trước khi viết, cần xác định chính xác đề đang hỏi gì. Ở đây, đề yêu cầu thảo luận hai quan điểm (1) giáo dục có thể giải quyết vấn đề đồ ăn nhanh và (2) giáo dục không thể, sau đó đưa ra ý kiến cá nhân về quan điểm nào thuyết phục hơn. Dành 2–3 phút lập dàn ý trước khi viết sẽ giúp định hướng rõ mỗi đoạn.

  • 2

    Thì và hòa hợp chủ ngữ - động từ.

    Các lỗi như "the rate is increase fastly" và "it lead to they" xuất hiện lặp lại. Cần luyện kỹ: Hiện tại đơn vs. hiện tại tiếp diễn, Cách dùng động từ sau trợ động từ (need to know, lead to them developing).

  • 3

    Độ chính xác trong cấu tạo từ.

    Nhiều lỗi do dùng sai loại từ: harmful (tính từ) ≠ harm (danh từ), rapidly ≠ fastly, illnesses ≠ ill. Nên học theo nhóm từ (noun/verb/adjective/adverb) để tránh nhầm lẫn.

  • 4

    Tránh dùng dạng rút gọn.

    "It's" → "It is" trong văn học thuật. Dạng rút gọn chỉ phù hợp với văn nói hoặc viết không trang trọng.
